Instructions

Rapport Explanation:

Rapport is a term used to describe a combination of qualities that emerge from a particular kind of interaction. These interactions are characterized by such statements as 'we really clicked' or 'we experienced real chemistry.' Terms like "engrossing," "friendly," "harmonious", "involving", and "worthwhile" describe interactions high in rapport. Your job is to rate the level of rapport for every video clip in the interactions we videotaped, which are between two students who are taking turns tutoring each other in algebra. The videos are shuffled, so each HIT you do will be a random slice of the interaction.

 

Submission Style:

Please rate the rapport in each 30 second video on a scale from 1 to 7, where 1 represents the complete absence of rapport, 4 is a neutral level of rapport, and 7 represents the highest possible rapport.

Please also rate your confidence in your rapport rating, again from a scale of 1 to 7, where 1 is the lowest confidence in your rating, and 7 is the highest confidence.

Note: If you hear or see a third person (an older experimenter) in the room at any point during the 30 seconds, OR if the participants leave the room at any point during the video, select N/A for both rapport and confidence, but continue to watch the video till the end.

 

You must watch the entirety of ALL videos, and rate all 10 videos to have your HIT accepted.

First, watch two positive, two negative, and two neutral examples, and see if your guess of the rapport rating matches our rating.

Then rate the 10 videos.

At the end, please put a brief explanation of what you think low, neutral, and high rapport look and sound like.
